HPT Glossary

Human Performance Technology (HPT)
A systematic approach to improving productivity and competence, using proven principles of instructional design, measurement, and management to analyze and improve human performance in organizations.
Performance Analysis
The systematic process of identifying performance gaps, analyzing their causes, and determining appropriate interventions to close those gaps.
Performance Gap
The difference between current performance and desired performance, often measured in terms of quality, quantity, timeliness, or cost-effectiveness.
Root Cause Analysis
A systematic approach to identifying the fundamental causes of performance problems rather than just addressing symptoms.
Intervention
A planned action or set of actions designed to address identified performance problems and close performance gaps.
Environmental Factors
External conditions and resources that influence performance, including information, tools, incentives, and organizational support.
ISPI
The International Society for Performance Improvement, the leading professional association for HPT practitioners worldwide.
Behavior Engineering Model (BEM)
Thomas Gilbert's framework for analyzing performance that examines six factors: information, resources, incentives, knowledge, capacity, and motives.
Worthy Performance
Performance that achieves valuable accomplishments with the least costly behavior, as defined by Thomas Gilbert.
Stakeholder
Any individual or group who has an interest in or is affected by a performance improvement initiative.
Needs Assessment
A systematic process for determining and addressing gaps between current conditions and desired conditions or requirements.
Return on Investment (ROI)
A financial measure used to evaluate the effectiveness of an investment, calculated as the ratio of net benefits to costs.
Systematic Approach
A methodical, organized way of addressing performance problems that follows established principles and procedures.
Performance Support
Resources and tools that help people perform effectively in their work environment, including job aids, documentation, and technology.
Change Management
The process of planning, implementing, and reinforcing changes in organizations to ensure successful adoption of new practices.
Performance Consultant
A professional who applies HPT principles to help organizations improve human performance through systematic analysis and intervention.
Front-End Analysis
The initial phase of HPT process that involves identifying performance problems, analyzing causes, and determining intervention strategies.
Evaluation
The systematic assessment of the effectiveness and impact of performance interventions, including formative and summative evaluation.
Organizational Development
A planned, systematic approach to improving organizational effectiveness through the application of behavioral science principles.
Performance Standards
Clear, measurable criteria that define acceptable levels of performance for specific tasks or roles.
