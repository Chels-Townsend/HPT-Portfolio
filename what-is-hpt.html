What is Human Performance Technology?

Definition of HPT

Human Performance Technology (HPT) is a systematic approach to improving productivity and competence, using a set of methods and procedures that realize the potential of people in the workplace. HPT is a field of study and practice that uses a systematic approach to analyze and improve human performance in organizations through the application of proven principles of instructional design, measurement, and management.

HPT practitioners use a systematic process to identify performance gaps, analyze their causes, and recommend interventions that will close those gaps while considering the organizational context, constraints, and desired outcomes. The field emphasizes evidence-based practice and measurable results, making it a valuable approach for organizations seeking to improve their human capital effectiveness.

International Society for Performance Improvement (ISPI)

The International Society for Performance Improvement (ISPI) is the leading professional association for HPT practitioners worldwide. Founded in 1962, ISPI is dedicated to improving individual, organizational, and societal performance through the application of performance technology principles and practices.

ISPI serves as the premier resource for HPT professionals, providing certification programs, research publications, professional development opportunities, and networking events. The organization maintains the highest standards for HPT practice through its certification programs and continues to advance the field through research and knowledge sharing.

ISPI Standards

ISPI has established comprehensive standards that define the knowledge, skills, and competencies required for effective HPT practice. These standards are organized into several key areas:

Performance Analysis Standards

These standards focus on the systematic analysis of performance gaps, including the ability to conduct needs assessments, cause analysis, and environmental analysis. Practitioners must demonstrate competency in identifying performance problems and their root causes.

Intervention Design Standards

These standards address the design and development of performance interventions, including instructional design, non-instructional solutions, and change management strategies. Practitioners must show proficiency in selecting and designing appropriate interventions.

Evaluation Standards

These standards cover the measurement and evaluation of performance interventions, including the design of evaluation strategies, data collection methods, and the interpretation of results to demonstrate impact and return on investment.

Management Standards

These standards address the management of HPT projects and initiatives, including project planning, resource allocation, stakeholder management, and the communication of results to various organizational levels.

Key Principles of HPT

HPT is grounded in several fundamental principles that guide practice: systematic thinking, evidence-based decision making, focus on results and accomplishments, consideration of multiple intervention options, and partnership with clients and stakeholders. These principles ensure that HPT practitioners approach performance improvement with rigor, objectivity, and a commitment to measurable outcomes.
